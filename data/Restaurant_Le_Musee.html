Reservations a Must
Feb 2020 • none
We tried to eat here a few years ago on the recommendation of our Beaujolais Nouveau tour organizer but full. This time, since restaurant doesn't have way to contact except by phone, I had my hotel make the reservations a few weeks in advance. Our reservation was for 1215 but we arrived at noon opening and were the first ones seated in the corner furthest from the door. It slowly began to fill and all but maybe one small table was eventually occupied. Many were turned away. There were mostly locals and us! There is no printed menu. The wine choices and prices are on various places throughout the restaurant-chalkboards and tables stands, etc. A bowl of fried lard is at each setting for a complimentary starter. The owner/chef comes around to each table and presents the menu and takes the order. We both started with 6 escargot dish with pastry puff in center. I had a beef stew dish for my main (it wasn't called boeuf bourgignon but was similar). My other half had the local pike fish specialty (Quenelles de Brochet), which is similar to a caserole with fish. We both received the same side dish with au gratin potatoes and a carrot/cabbage combo. For dessert, he had the chocolatge mousse and I had the mousse/tart combo, which was a 1/2 portion of each so I got to taste both! Overall, the price is so reasonable for everything-about 63 € with wine. This will absolutely be the #1 choice on our future visits to Lyon, if I can get a reservation, for dinner and lunch. If you can't get in here, my second choice is La Maison Marie, but it fills up too! Bon Appetit….

Best Bouchon in Lyon
Jan 2023 • couples
We returned here for our 3rd visit but 2nd time dining. The 1st time we didn't have reservations so were turned away but with a smile. We had lunch a few years ago and dinner this time. We both chose the 32€ menu (Menu Soir 32). We received an Amuse Bouche to start (Pork Skins in a small bowl). My other half had Butternut Squash soup, Chicken w Crayfish, and Mousse au Chocolat. I had the Escargot appetizer, the local fish specialty, Quenelle de Brochet, and Tarte a la Praline. We each had house wine: me 1-46cl Rhone red wine plus 1-25cl as well and other half 1-46cl Viognier white wine. 46cl is 14€,and 25cl is 6,50€?? This is the best bouchon in Lyon; one can tell its quality by the demand. I now email our hotel weeks in advance to make a reservation since they only take reservations over the phone or in person! The menu is done orally by the owner as he visits each table. Since most bouchons mainly serve the same choices (at least 90% of the menus are the same), I think that this will be the only one we visit in the future. The other bouchons we've visited have one or more issues: 1) no house wine so overpriced bottles only 2) lack of personal touches, 3) food quality not as good, and 4) so many more. Please visit here, arrive at opening time whether for lunch or dinner, and most importantly, MAKE reservation in ADVANCE! Merci encore. Also, note the wall with postcards and pictures!!

The best of real Lyonnais corks
May 2023 • business
We regularly go to the Museum for dinner with my colleagues. The food is simple but absolutely delicious, exactly what you would expect from a cork. The staff is friendly and very efficient, with of course a special mention for the boss Luc who combines the art of cooking and that of theater, you are at the show for the (very reasonable!) price of the meal. If he is in a good mood that day you can even be entitled to a short guided tour of the surrounding traboules at the end of your meal, very nice! The restaurant is small and the tables are very crowded, but that's part of the corky atmosphere! The only downsides are that this restaurant is a victim of its own success and you must therefore reserve well in advance, and moreover for weekends it is only open on Saturdays at noon which does not make visits easy when you're not from Lyon. It's always a pleasure to come and dine "at Luc's", and I plan to return there often!
