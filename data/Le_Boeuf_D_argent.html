Best restaurant in Lyon with incredible service and a wonderful atmosphere. Deserving of a Michelin star
May 2022 • couples
This restaurant deserves a 10/5 for their incredible service, delicious food, relaxing atmosphere, and amazing overall experience. Another restaurant booked me at the wrong location and I was on my honeymoon, all dressed up and ready for a fancy dinner with no plans, left hung out to dry. I found le bœuf close by and approached the door. The sweet server at le bœuf asked the owner if they could accommodate us even though they were fully booked. The owner rearranged some tables and made it work—we were incredibly grateful. The aperitif was a nice sparking wine and we kicked things off with an amuse-bouche which featured a slim orange slice that was a refreshing base and a scallop cake on the side. I had the langoustine starter which offered whole langoustine and cream of langoustine with a beetroot and cranberry sauce. They had the most tender and flavorful scallops atop a thing sliced carrot and polenta. Paired well with a nice white wine. I then tried the lyonnaise classic Cervelle de Canut which was packed with fresh herbs and not garlicky like it sometimes is served. To finish, a coffee tartlet with pecan pieces and a brûlée topped mousse. It was perhaps the greatest dessert I have ever had. On top of all this, the prices are absolutely reasonable and I believe this establishment deserves a Michelin Star. I am forever grateful that this wonderful, quaint, family establishment in old town Lyon went so far out of their way to turn our night around on our honeymoon. This will be a must visit for us every time we are in Lyon.

Great Restaurant, Price and Service
Mar 2024 • couples
My wife and I wanted to try good french cooking with a touch of Lyonnaise style, since this was our first visit to Lyon. The restaurant was great, and included an Italian touch as a bonus. From the street we descended a few steps into a comfortable and welcoming dining area. It had a relaxed, friendly feel to it. The waitstaff, professional and courteous, knew our reservation name without reference to notes and seated us immediately. The decor is warm. Vaulted ceilings, cream walls and enough decoration to feel like a good restaurant without being overwhelming. The tables were a comfortable size, with plenty of room for drinks and meals. Service was fast, friendly and knowledgeable. Drink orders were taken quickly and the menu provided. Once the orders were taken an amuse bouche was quickly on the table and from then on the meal was well-paced. At no stage did we feel rushed, yet there was no waiting for clearing the table or between courses; or, at least, no more than necessary for us to enjoy ourselves. The menu has a number of fixed menus, of various complexities and costs. Although there was a note that the menu was for the whole table there was no problem ordering a different fixed menu for each of us. We were not very hungry so chose a simple menu. We were glad of that, though, as the quality and quantity were just right. In our case we particularly enjoyed the beetroot carpaccio and our different main courses of duck and roast octopus. We had chosen different courses, each, so that we could each try them both. Perfectly cooked, with original touches in the sauces and accompaniments, they gave the pleasure of those little 'Wow' moments that are so important in a good meal. We also really enjoyed the cheese selection with the unusual savoury jam and the lavender ice cream for pre -dessert. As we were leaving, the chef came out to ask if we had enjoyed the meal and to get feedback. I had seen her do this with other diners and it was a lovely touch to finish a very enjoyable meal. This a new favourite for us.
