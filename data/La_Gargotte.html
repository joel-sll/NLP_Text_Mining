Ivan Peric, a prince of cuisine
Feb 2023 • friends
I know that she lived for 35 years on rue Royale, this street which was once at the heart of Lyon life - it was through this street that the kings of France entered Lyon, hence its name -, the street Royale continues to exist thanks to some big names in cuisine. And among these names, that of Ivan Peric is one of those that we have been following for a long time with the same greed. Far from being a restaurant, this restaurant, which was once a renowned pastry shop, is both an elegant and relaxed place, chic and friendly, as all chef's bistros want to be today. Except Ivan, he's not pretending. He is naturally warm and talented. A charismatic man, a real character and an outstanding chef whose career is marked by stints in remarkable houses, like that of Marc Veyrat, or in the private kitchens of the greats of the world, notably in Saint-Tropez. What is all the more remarkable is that Ivan continues year after year to delight us with creative cuisine, prepared daily with fresh produce. Only open in the evening, a menu also unique with a generosity equal to that of the cook. A mixture of flavors and textures, the audacity and know-how of mastered contemporary cuisine... Without doubt one of the best value for money in the city.

What a fabulous dining experience! We visited La Gargotte on the last night of our vacation, along with friends from the area. We were all blown away! In addition to the extensive wine list, there were some specialty champagne cocktails (delicious). We were pleasantly surprised when the owner/chef came out to greet us soon after we arrived. Our party all selected the “Menu complet”, which included a choice of 4 starters, main courses, and desserts. The starters were wonderful, from the salmon with ricotta, to the beef tataki, and the portions were fairly large. The main courses received rave reviews as well: the duck with mustard sauce and the cod fillet were prepared beautifully. While we were all fairly full at this point, we somehow managed to make room for the desserts, which included a divine limoncello crème brûlée, a wonderful chocolate confection, and an assortment of speciality cheeses. Chef Ivan even took the time to chat with us and bid us farewell. You can tell he loves what he does, and it shows in every dish that is served. What an unbelievable meal at such a reasonable price. Whether you are local or just visiting, do not miss this hidden gem!
